Our History

From humble beginnings to a legacy of excellence, discover the inspiring journey of Engreg Schools.

The Story of Engreg Schools

In 1987, Mrs. Evelyn Nwamaka Orekie was lecturing on Early Childhood Education in the Professional Service in Education (PSE) program at the University of Lagos. During one of her sessions, some students disputed a theory she presented, arguing that it would only be effective in affluent areas like Victoria Island, Ikeja, and Surulere, but not in low-income communities like Ajegunle and Somolu.

Determined to prove them wrong, Mrs. Orekie took on the challenge. She secured a space at No. 1 Apata Street, Shomolu, and founded Saint Gregory Nursery School. After operating there for three years, the school relocated to a larger space at No. 9 Apata Street and transitioned into Engreg International School.

In 1993, the school received operational approval from the Lagos State Ministry of Education. In 2000, after 13 years of operating in rented facilities, the school moved to its current purpose-built site at No. 24/26 Bankole Street, Pedro, Shomolu, Lagos, where it now offers three levels of education: Kindergarten, Primary, and Secondary School.

Community Impact and Vision

On October 1, 2010, during Nigeria's Golden Jubilee celebration, the entire school community—students, staff, and the proprietress—participated in a community service project on Bankole Street, reflecting the school's deep commitment to its host community.

Beyond its immediate environment, Engreg Schools have extended support and fellowship to several institutions and communities, including Pedro Police Station, Home People's Home at Mushin, Lagos, Modupe Cole Special Home, and several Motherless Children's Homes.
